Reference Panel · Accuracy & Agreement cohort -

Computed live from the benchmark workspace. For judgment-heavy records there is often no single correct score, only a distribution of defensible expert judgments. The engine (rung 2) is evaluated by whether it behaves like a competent member of that distribution, not by matching one answer key. Agreement among the experts is itself a primary metric, reported with its sample size. Reviewer agreement (reliability, not a rung) measures how often independent reviewers reach the same determination. Rung 3 (whether a JRS verdict predicts real-world outcomes) is separate; real cases are accruing, with no validity figure reported until the screened-case minimum is met. All de-identified.

Every figure carries its N and the cohort version it was computed against. Distribution statistics are withheld as “insufficient expert data” until the configured minimum number of experts is reached; the gate fails closed if the minimum is unset. The panel is described as a benchmark only when expert-to-expert agreement reaches the configured threshold, otherwise as a reference panel (alignment with informed professional judgment, not correctness against a single truth). Expert opinions are never synthesized or interpolated to fill a sparse distribution, and ranges are never widened to make the engine fall inside them. Thresholds are placeholders pending the study owner.
