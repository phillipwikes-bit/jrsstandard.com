The Stakes

The record becomes the only witness.

People leave. Memories shift. Outcomes change, and an investigation begins. The decision survives in the file; the reasoning behind it usually does not. By the time anyone asks, the record is all that remains.

Auditors ask. Regulators ask. Boards ask. Attorneys ask. The question is rarely “what was the outcome.” It is “why was the decision made, and what supported it at the time.”

JRS examines whether a record can answer that question, before it is finalized.

What the five conditions ask, in plain terms

Each JRS condition maps to a question a reviewer, a regulator, or a board actually asks.

Basis Identification
Why was the decision made?
Decision-Process Traceability
How was it reached?
Evidentiary Sufficiency
What supported it?
Chronology
What happened, and when?
Reconstructability
Can someone understand it years later?

The fastest-growing version of this gap is the AI-assisted record: drafted by a model, finalized by a person, and filed with the reasoning stripped out. That is where this preview begins.

02 · The Blind Spot

The Draft-to-Documentation Gap

Model-layer governance examines what the model produces. The moment that output is handed to a person, edited, and finalized, model-layer visibility drops to zero. The record that carries the organization's exposure is created entirely inside that blind spot. The control coverage and the liability sit in different places.

Step 1
AI output
Model-layer governance is present here.
Step 2
Human edit
Governance visibility drops to zero.
Step 3
Sign-off
No documentation control present.
Step 4
Permanent record
Liability is now held here.

JRS examines the record at Step 3, before finalization: a documentation safety control in the place where model-layer coverage ends.

03 · Definition

What JRS Is, and Is Not

JRS Is
—A documentation safety control
—A pre-finalization review structure
—An operational validation program
—A governance-compatible layer that examines the finalized record
JRS Is Not
×Armor for the model
×A band-aid over a model problem
×A model-monitoring competitor
×A finished or validated product

Coexistence, not competition. Partners handle technology safety controls. JRS handles documentation safety controls. The two layers examine different surfaces and do not overlap.

04 · Live Demonstration

How JRS Examines a Record

JRS reviews a record against five conditions and documents a structured finding. The interface below shows that flow.

The five conditions
01
Reconstructability
The conclusion can be reconstructed from the file alone, by a later reviewer with no access to the author and no institutional memory.
02
Basis Identification
The basis for each conclusion is identifiable within the record.
03
Chronology
The sequence of events can be followed from the record.
04
Decision-Process Traceability
The decision process that led to the conclusion can be traced.
05
Evidentiary Sufficiency
The evidence in the record is sufficient to support the conclusion.

05 · Integration

How JRS Snaps Into a Partner Stack

JRS adds a documentation review step at the point of finalization and feeds a structured record asset into a partner's existing audit trail. It makes a stack more complete; it replaces nothing.

01

Pre-finalization hook

At sign-off, the draft record is passed to JRS, which evaluates it against the five conditions and returns a structured finding before the record is committed.

02

Record-asset schema

JRS returns a compact, inspectable record asset the partner stores alongside its own artifacts.

03

Evidence feed into the partner audit trail

Each review documents an append-only entry that the partner surfaces inside its existing audit log. JRS adds a record-layer signal the partner does not currently capture.

Record-asset schema · design artifact
record_asset {
  record_id            : uuid
  source_ai_function   : summarization | recommendation | analysis | narrative
  finalized_by         : actor_ref
  finalized_at         : timestamp
  conditions {
    basis_identification        : pass | review | gap
    reasoning_traceability      : pass | review | gap
    cold_reviewer_clarity       : pass | review | gap
    accountability_support      : pass | review | gap
    temporal_reconstructability : pass | review | gap
  }
  determination        : ready | review_required | gap_identified
  remediation_note     : text
  reviewer_ref         : actor_ref
  review_timestamp     : timestamp
  engine_version       : semver
}
Audit-log entry · append-only design
audit_entry {
  entry_id, record_id, determination, conditions_summary,
  engine_version, reviewed_at, prev_entry_hash
}  // append-only; entries are added, never edited
Developer Reference · Review Engine API

A partner engineer wires the call below into the finalization step and stores the returned record asset in the existing audit trail. The engine is live today for sandbox and pilot integration. Full machine-readable contract: OpenAPI spec.

•

Endpoint

POST https://www.jrsstandard.com/api/v1/review-engine. The endpoint is versioned and CORS-enabled. Body: { "text": "<record>", "runs": 1 } (runs 1 to 5; runs>1 returns disclosed variance).

•

Authentication

A bearer token is required for partner and pilot integration. Send Authorization: Bearer <token>. Distinct tokens are issued per partner. Open sandbox mode is available separately for evaluation.

•

Request correlation & limits

Every response carries a request_id (also in the X-Request-Id header) for audit correlation. A best-effort per-IP rate limit applies; production volumes are arranged per partner.

Sample request · curl
curl -X POST https://www.jrsstandard.com/api/v1/review-engine \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer <token-if-enabled>" \
  -d '{"text":"<record text, 40+ chars>","runs":3}'
Sample client · JavaScript
async function jrsReview(text, runs) {
  const res = await fetch("https://www.jrsstandard.com/api/v1/review-engine", {
    method: "POST",
    headers: {
      "Content-Type": "application/json"
      // , "Authorization": "Bearer <token-if-enabled>"
    },
    body: JSON.stringify({ text, runs: runs || 1 })
  });
  const data = await res.json();          // record-asset result (+ variance if runs>1)
  // store data.result + data.request_id in your audit trail at finalization
  return data;
}
Error codes
400 record_too_short | invalid_json    422-style input issues
401 unauthorized                       (only when tokens are enabled)
405 method_not_allowed                 (use POST)
429 rate_limited                       (best-effort per-IP)
502 review_failed                      (model or parse error)
503 engine_not_configured              (engine key absent)

Stage note: the engine is in operational validation and is unvalidated. determination is derived by rule from the five condition statuses; the statuses come from a single model and vary across runs, which the variance block discloses. Reproducibility is distinct from accuracy and from validation.

07 · Fit by Platform Category

Where the Gap Sits in Each Stack

Each category below leads with what its coverage does not reach, then what JRS adds at the record layer.

AI-Governance Record Systems
Coverage reaches the model and its outputs; it does not reach the human-edited record after sign-off.
JRS adds a pre-finalization review and a record asset that carries audit-record DNA into the existing trail.
HR-Tech Documentation Platforms
Workflow and storage are covered; whether a finalized record identifies the basis for its conclusions is not examined.
JRS adds a documentation review at sign-off that surfaces reviewability gaps before the record is filed.
GRC Ecosystems
Controls and policies are tracked; the reviewability of the underlying records is assumed, not assessed.
JRS adds a record-level signal that feeds the control evidence base GRC already aggregates.
Legal-Tech / E-Discovery
Records are searched and produced as they exist; whether a record holds up read cold is out of scope.
JRS adds a pre-finalization examination of temporal reconstructability, upstream of discovery.
Model-Testing / Compliance Platforms
Model behavior is tested; the human-finalized document the model contributed to is not reviewed.
JRS adds the documentation-layer counterpart to model-layer testing.
The Common Thread
In every category, coverage ends at the model or the workflow.
JRS occupies the record layer beneath all of them: coexistence, not replacement.
