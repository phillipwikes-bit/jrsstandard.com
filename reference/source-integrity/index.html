Source Integrity (Review Against Original Notes)

Short Explanation

Source Integrity is the requirement that the record faithfully reflect its underlying notes. The reference states the check directly: generated or drafted wording is reviewed against the original notes before review, no characterizations appear in the record that are absent from the underlying notes, and where the notes contained gaps or conflicts the record reflects them rather than smoothing them over. The concern is sharpest for AI-assisted drafting, where generated prose can introduce findings, certainty, or attributions the source notes never contained. Human attestation that this comparison occurred is documented and on file before submission. Source Integrity asks whether the record can be trusted against the material it claims to rest on, not merely whether it reads well.

Why It Matters

AI-generated summaries can introduce characterizations not present in the original notes. When a record enters a personnel or case file, those introduced characterizations become the basis for escalation and proceedings, and they fail the moment anyone compares the record to the notes.

Reviewer Questions

  • Was the generated or drafted wording reviewed against the original notes before this review?
  • Do any characterizations appear in the record that are absent from the underlying notes?
  • Where the notes contained gaps or conflicts, does the record reflect them rather than smooth them over?
  • Is human attestation documented and on file before submission?

Common Failure Pattern

AI-generated wording is submitted to a file without review against the original notes. A characterization not present in the notes hardens into a finding that cannot be supported.
