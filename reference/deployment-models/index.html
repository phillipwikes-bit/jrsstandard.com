Deployment Models

Short Explanation

Deployment Models describe how JRS is adopted inside an existing environment without system change or dedicated staff. An individual reviewer applies the conditions to the next records in their own queue. A small team applies the conditions independently to the same records and then compares where determinations diverged. A simulation-first deployment calibrates reviewers on constructed scenarios before they touch live records. A departmental deployment applies the conditions to a single record population over a defined cycle. The models share a deliberate property: participation can begin small, scale to what is useful, and pause or expand based on what it surfaces. JRS is a review structure that operates within current workflows, so deployment is a matter of where review attention is directed, not of installing or replacing anything. The model chosen depends on the team's size, record population, and tolerance for coordination.

Why It Matters

Adoption fails when it demands restructuring before it has shown value. Lightweight deployment models let an organization test documentation review against its own records at low commitment and expand only where it proves useful.

Reviewer Questions

  • Which deployment model fits this team's size and record population?
  • Can participation begin with a single reviewer or one record type?
  • Is calibration happening before reviewers apply the conditions to live records?
  • Can the deployment pause or expand based on what it surfaces?

Common Failure Pattern

An organization attempts a broad rollout before reviewers are calibrated, produces inconsistent determinations, and concludes the framework does not work when the deployment, not the framework, was the problem.
