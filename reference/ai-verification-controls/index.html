AI Verification Controls

Short Explanation

AI Verification Controls are the checks a person applies to AI-assisted content before a record is finalized. They target the known failure modes directly: confirm that each attribution corresponds to a real source that supports the claim, that qualified facts have not been strengthened into declarations, that conflicts and gaps in the source material remain visible rather than smoothed away, and that specifics such as dates and names survived drafting. The controls operate at the last point where the failure modes can be caught: the human edit and approval step, where a person takes ownership of text a model produced. JRS does not treat AI assistance as disqualifying; it treats AI-assisted records as records that require a verification step proportionate to the way generative text degrades. The controls are deliberately concrete so they can be applied without specialist tooling.

Why It Matters

AI-assisted content enters permanent records at the moment a human approves it. That approval is both the point of greatest risk, because generated text looks finished, and the point of greatest leverage, because a defined verification step can catch the predictable failure modes before they are committed.

Reviewer Questions

  • Has each AI-introduced attribution been verified against a real, supporting source?
  • Were any qualified facts strengthened into declarations during drafting?
  • Do conflicts and gaps in the source material remain visible in the record?
  • Did specifics (dates, names, evidence) survive the drafting process?

Common Failure Pattern

A reviewer approves AI-assisted text without verification. The overstatement or false attribution enters the permanent record and is discovered only when the underlying material is examined.
