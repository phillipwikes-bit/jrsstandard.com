Where the Evidence Stands

Updated 2026-06-20 · Operational validation phase

JRS is evaluated on a three-rung evidence ladder: reproducibility, then accuracy against a panel of expert reviewers, then validation against real-world outcomes. The figures below are computed from the live research workspace. JRS is in operational validation and makes no claim of validated effectiveness. The samples are small and the numbers are working signals, not validated metrics.

Rung 1 · Reproducibility

A nightly study asks several AI models, one per vendor, to judge the same constructed records, and measures how often they reach the same determination. Consistency is not accuracy.

The latest run was cross-vendor, with two of three vendors returning a usable answer. Different vendors reach the same JRS determination roughly three times in four to nine times in ten, night to night, on three constructed records. This is a reproducibility signal on a very small record set.

Rung 2 · Accuracy against the reference panel

A panel of expert reviewers scores a shared set of records. The engine is evaluated by whether it behaves like a competent member of that panel, not by matching a single answer key. Agreement among the experts is itself a primary metric.

On the shared set of five records, three experts agree with one another at a chance-corrected level of 0.75. The engine lands inside the experts' range on every record, and agrees with the experts at least as often as the experts agree with one another, with nothing flagged out of range. This is a strong early result on five records and a three-person panel: a working demonstration, not proof.

Reviewer reliability

Separate from the expert panel, regular reviewers score the same records. How often independent reviewers reach the same determination is a measurement-quality property, not one of the three rungs.

Ten regular reviewers scored the set, averaging 75% agreement. Reviewers agree strongly on the vague records, where the gap is obvious, and split evenly on the two well-documented records, where the question is whether a complete-looking file is ready or still needs work. That split is the point at which a documentation standard, and an expert panel, earns its place.

Panel composition

The reference panel currently spans three professional domains. Expert identities are held privately by design and are not published here.

The expert panel is at its minimum of three. Three experts support a working demonstration, not a published claim. Strengthening Rung 2 calls for more experts, with several in each domain under review, and more records across domains. Regular reviewers are already sufficient in number.

What this is, and is not

These figures describe consistency and alignment with informed professional judgment during an operational validation phase. They are not validated effectiveness, legal sufficiency, or a guarantee of any outcome. The first independent professional evaluation is scheduled to complete in August 2026.

Methodology: the three-rung ladder, the reference-panel model, and the gating thresholds are documented in the research data room. The live figures are recomputed from the workspace; this page is a dated snapshot.
