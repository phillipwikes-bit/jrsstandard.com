Training Modules
Module 01What Pre-Finalization Review Is and Why It Matters
expand

Records are reviewed later by people who were not present during the original events. The reviewer may have inherited the file mid-process, joined the organization after the fact, or be reading the record months or years after it was created.

Pre-finalization review addresses a specific gap: documentation that appears complete when drafted may not be reconstructable later. What felt obvious during drafting may not survive the handover.

Core principle

Review occurs before the record enters the official system. That is the only point where a gap can be resolved without consequence. Once a record is filed, the opportunity has passed.

The review checks three things: can the record stand on its own, does each conclusion have a specific anchor in the file, and can a later reviewer reconstruct the basis without contacting the original author.

The five questions applied before any record enters the system:

1
Can this record stand on its own?A later reviewer understands the basis without calling the original author.
2
What specific evidence anchors each conclusion?Dates, logs, documented interactions, or referenced records on file.
3
Is the path from evidence to conclusion visible in the record?Not in the author's memory. In the document itself.
4
Are dates, timelines, and policies identifiable?Missing dates are the most common single documentation gap.
5
Were AI-assisted characterizations confirmed against source material?Human confirmation required before system entry.
Module 02Identifying Common Documentation Failure Modes
expand

The following failure modes appear repeatedly across record types. Each represents a gap that is rarely obvious at drafting time but becomes visible when a later reviewer reads the file without context.

Failure Mode 1: Evaluative Adjectives Without Behavioral Anchors

Before
"Employee has demonstrated unprofessional and hostile behavior toward colleagues."
▶ No specific conduct described. No dates. No documentation of what occurred.
After
On April 4 and April 11, employee interrupted two client meetings after receiving written instruction on March 28 regarding meeting protocols (on file). Incident report dated April 12 covers both incidents.

Failure Mode 2: Pattern Claims Without Dates

Before
"Employee has been repeatedly absent without advance notice on multiple occasions."
▶ "Repeatedly" and "multiple occasions" — no dates. Pattern cannot be independently established.
After
Employee was absent without advance notice on January 5, January 12, and February 3, contrary to the Attendance Policy acknowledged January 2. Counseling note issued February 4 on file.

Failure Mode 3: Escalation Without a Prior Counseling Trail

Before
"After repeated coaching and progressive discipline, employee has failed to demonstrate improvement necessary to continue employment."
▶ "Repeated coaching" — no coaching notes. "Progressive discipline" — no prior records. Record arrived without any referenced documentation.
After
Employee placed on Performance Improvement Plan January 10. Check-in notes from January 24, February 14, March 3 reflect continued gaps. Written warning issued March 6. Employee acknowledged PIP and warning in writing. HR secondary review completed March 12. All records on file.
From practice

Most records that fail during later review looked complete to the person who drafted them. The gap is usually not intent. It is the distance between what the author understood and what the file actually shows.

Module 03Secondary Review — When to Escalate and How
expand

Secondary review is triggered when a high-risk indicator is present in the record. Any single indicator is sufficient reason to route for secondary review before system entry.

Trigger
Evaluative language without a specific behavioral anchor
Trigger
Pattern conduct claim without specific dated instances
Trigger
Escalation without a documented prior counseling trail
Trigger
Termination without referenced supporting documentation
Trigger
AI-assisted wording not reviewed against source notes
Trigger
Accommodation record missing interactive process documentation

When a trigger is identified, complete the Secondary Review Escalation Form before the record advances. The form documents the gap, the action required, and the routing disposition.

!
STOPEvaluative language without anchor. Missing timeline. Do not submit. Return to drafter.
~
REVIEWBasis likely accurate but not fully visible. Secondary review or clarification needed before entry.
✓
READYSelf-contained. Evidence identifiable. Reasoning traceable. Basis holds without explanation from the author.
Reviewer note

Secondary review cannot repair unsupported drafting. Records that arrive without a traceable basis are returned to the drafter — not approved with a notation. Returning a record is the correct action.

Module 04AI-Assisted Documentation Review
expand

Where AI tools contributed to a record, the review standard does not change. The review evaluates whether the basis is visible in the file regardless of how the wording was generated.

Semantic Inflation

AI-assisted drafting may introduce language that appears procedurally complete despite limited underlying evidentiary support. Common patterns: qualified language in source notes becomes declarative in the generated record; general impressions are stated as findings; conflicts in source material are smoothed over rather than preserved.

AI Summary — Before
"Employee has consistently demonstrated difficulty working within team structures and shown resistance to supervisory guidance."
▶ Source records not identified. "Consistently demonstrated" — no dates, no behavioral anchors. "Resistance" — not traceable to documented interactions.
After Source Review
Summary reviewed against meeting notes dated March 4, 11, and 18, and coaching records February 10 and March 3. On March 4, employee declined project planning participation without stated reason (meeting notes). On March 11, employee did not submit required status report despite two documented requests. Human review completed March 19. Confirmed against original notes. No conflicts identified.

The five-step AI review verification sequence:

1
Source IdentificationList specific source records reviewed before drafting. Dates required.
2
Characterization ReviewEach characterization traced to a specific documented interaction.
3
Conflict and Gap CheckConflicting accounts preserved. Gaps noted, not smoothed over.
4
Human ConfirmationReviewer confirms wording against source notes. Confirmation documented before system entry.
5
System EntryRecord enters official system with confirmation on file. Source records identified.
Reviewer test

If the source notes were removed, would the record still appear equally well-supported? If yes, the record may contain semantic inflation. The supporting basis exists in the wording but not in the underlying file.

Module 05Investigation Review and Conflicting Account Preservation
expand

Investigation records present specific reconstruction challenges. The conclusion must be traceable to identified source materials and the reasoning must be visible within the record itself.

Conflicting Account Preservation

Where accounts conflict, the conflict should remain visible in the record. Resolving conflicts through omission creates a gap that surfaces during later review.

Problematic Approach
"Based on the available evidence, the investigator found that the incident occurred as described by the complainant."
▶ Respondent account absent. Basis for crediting one account not stated. Conflicting evidence not acknowledged.
Supported Approach
Complainant provided contemporaneous documentation dated April 3. Respondent denied the conduct and provided no supporting documentation. One witness corroborated complainant's account; one was not present. Credibility determination based on contemporaneous documentation and corroboration. Respondent's account noted and preserved.

Investigation review flow:

1
Complaint IntakeParties, allegations, and dates recorded before investigation opens.
2
Source CollectionWitnesses identified. Documents gathered. Each source logged.
3
Timeline ReconstructionEvents sequenced from documented sources. Gaps identified and noted.
4
Conflicting Account PreservationAccounts that conflict with the finding remain visible. Not resolved away.
5
Finding ReviewConclusion traced to identified sources. Reasoning visible in record.
6
Final DocumentationRecord filed with sources identified, conflicts noted, reasoning visible from file alone.
Investigator note

The investigation record must stand without the investigator available to explain it. If following the reasoning requires knowing what the investigator knew, the record is not complete.

Module 06Implementation — Starting and Expanding Review Practices
expand

JRS is designed for incremental adoption. No policy change, system integration, or formal rollout is required. Start with one record type. Expand from demonstrated usefulness.

1
Identify one record type to start withTermination records, accommodation documentation, or investigation conclusions are highest-leverage. Performance records are the most common entry point.
2
Share the Redlined Examples with drafters and reviewersWork through the before-and-after examples. Identify which failure modes appear most in your record types.
3
Apply the Pre-Finalization WorksheetDrafter or first-line reviewer applies the five questions before submission. The worksheet is self-explanatory.
4
Apply the Escalation Form when a high-risk indicator appearsAny single trigger is sufficient reason to route for secondary review before system entry.
5
Introduce the AI Verification ChecklistApply before any AI-assisted content enters an official system. Human confirmation documented before system entry.
6
Expand to additional record typesOnce established for one record type, introduce to other elevated-risk categories. Incremental. No structural change required.
Implementation note

Most implementation problems occur when review is introduced as a policy requirement before practitioners find it operationally useful. Start with one record type and one team. Expand from there.
