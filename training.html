Training Modules
Module 01What Pre-Finalization Review Is and Why It Matters
expand

Pre-finalization review is applied at the drafting stage, before a record enters an official system. The purpose is not to evaluate whether the underlying decision was correct. The purpose is to evaluate whether the basis for that decision is visible in the file.

Records are reviewed later by people who were not present during the original events. Staffing changes. People leave. The file outlasts the participants. What felt obvious during drafting may not be recoverable later.

Core principle

The review evaluates whether the support is in the file — not whether the outcome was correct. A record can be well-supported and still reflect a decision someone disagrees with. Documentation quality and decision quality are separate questions.

The Three Review Checks

Before finalizing any record, apply three tests:

1
The Time Traveler TestCan this record stand on its own? If someone unfamiliar with these events reviewed it two years from now, could they reconstruct why this decision was made without calling the original author?
2
The Fact AnchorWhat specific evidence supports each conclusion? A date, a log entry, a documented interaction, a referenced record. If the support is not in the document, it is not available for later review.
3
Documented ReasoningIs the path from evidence to conclusion visible within the record itself? The reasoning must be in the document — not in the author's memory.
From practice

Most records that fail during later review looked complete to the person who drafted them. The gap is usually not intent. It is the distance between what the author understood and what the file actually shows.

Module 02Identifying Common Documentation Failure Modes
expand

Documentation failures share a common characteristic: the record looks complete at the time of drafting, but a later reviewer with no prior knowledge of the events cannot evaluate what it says.

Failure Mode 1: Unsupported Evaluative Language

Before
"Employee has a negative attitude and is difficult to work with."
▶ No behavioral anchor. No dates. No documented interactions. A later reviewer cannot verify what conduct occurred.
After
Employee responded to three documented instructions with "I don't see why this is necessary" (Feb 2, Feb 9, Feb 16 meeting notes). Supervisor feedback provided February 17 and on file.

Failure Mode 2: Pattern Claims Without Dates

Before
"Employee has been repeatedly absent without advance notice on multiple occasions."
▶ "Repeatedly" and "multiple occasions" — no dates. Pattern cannot be independently established.
After
Employee was absent without advance notice on January 5, January 12, and February 3, contrary to the Attendance Policy acknowledged January 2. Counseling note issued February 4 on file.

Failure Mode 3: Escalation Without a Prior Counseling Trail

Before
"After repeated coaching and progressive discipline, employee has failed to demonstrate improvement necessary to continue employment."
▶ "Repeated coaching" — no coaching notes. "Progressive discipline" — no prior records referenced.
After
Employee placed on Performance Improvement Plan January 10. Check-in notes from January 24, February 14, March 3 reflect continued gaps. Written warning issued March 6. All records on file.
From practice

Most records that fail during later review looked complete to the person who drafted them. The gap is usually not intent. It is the distance between what the author understood and what the file actually shows.

Module 03Secondary Review — When to Escalate and How
expand

Secondary review is triggered when a high-risk indicator is present in the record. Any single indicator is sufficient reason to route for secondary review before system entry.

Trigger
Evaluative language without a specific behavioral anchor
Trigger
Pattern conduct claim without specific dated instances
Trigger
Escalation without a documented prior counseling trail
Trigger
Termination without referenced supporting documentation
Trigger
AI-assisted wording not reviewed against source notes
Trigger
Accommodation record missing interactive process documentation
!
STOPEvaluative language without anchor. Missing timeline. Do not submit. Return to drafter.
~
REVIEWBasis likely accurate but not fully visible. Secondary review or clarification needed before entry.
✓
READYSelf-contained. Evidence identifiable. Reasoning traceable. Basis holds without explanation from the author.
Reviewer note

Secondary review cannot repair unsupported drafting. Records that arrive without a traceable basis are returned to the drafter — not approved with a notation.

Module 04AI-Assisted Documentation Review
expand

Where AI tools contributed to a record, the review standard does not change. The review evaluates whether the basis is visible in the file regardless of how the wording was generated.

Semantic Inflation

AI-assisted drafting may introduce language that appears procedurally complete despite limited underlying evidentiary support. Qualified language in source notes becomes declarative in the generated record. General impressions are stated as findings. Conflicts in source material are smoothed over rather than preserved.

AI Summary — Before
"Employee has consistently demonstrated difficulty working within team structures and shown resistance to supervisory guidance."
▶ Source records not identified. No dates, no behavioral anchors. Not traceable to documented interactions.
After Source Review
Summary reviewed against meeting notes dated March 4, 11, and 18, and coaching records February 10 and March 3. On March 4, employee declined project planning participation without stated reason. On March 11, employee did not submit required status report despite two documented requests. Human review completed March 19. No conflicts identified.
1
Source IdentificationList specific source records reviewed before drafting. Dates required.
2
Characterization ReviewEach characterization traced to a specific documented interaction.
3
Conflict and Gap CheckConflicting accounts preserved. Gaps noted, not smoothed over.
4
Human ConfirmationReviewer confirms wording against source notes before system entry.
5
System EntryRecord enters official system with confirmation on file.
Module 05Investigation Review and Conflicting Account Preservation
expand

Investigation records present specific reconstruction challenges. The conclusion must be traceable to identified source materials and the reasoning must be visible within the record itself.

Problematic Approach
"Based on the available evidence, the investigator found that the incident occurred as described by the complainant."
▶ Respondent account absent. Basis for crediting one account not stated. Conflicting evidence not acknowledged.
Supported Approach
Complainant provided contemporaneous documentation dated April 3. Respondent denied the conduct and provided no supporting documentation. One witness corroborated complainant's account. Credibility determination based on contemporaneous documentation and corroboration. Respondent's account noted and preserved.
Investigator note

The investigation record must stand without the investigator available to explain it. If following the reasoning requires knowing what the investigator knew, the record is not complete.

Module 06Implementation — Starting and Expanding Review Practices
expand

JRS is designed for incremental adoption. No policy change, system integration, or formal rollout is required. Start with one record type. Expand from demonstrated usefulness.

1
Identify one record type to start withTermination records, accommodation documentation, or investigation conclusions are highest-leverage.
2
Share the Redlined Examples with drafters and reviewersWork through the before-and-after examples. Identify which failure modes appear most in your record types.
3
Apply the Pre-Finalization WorksheetDrafter or first-line reviewer applies the five questions before submission.
4
Apply the Escalation Form when a high-risk indicator appearsAny single trigger is sufficient reason to route for secondary review before system entry.
5
Introduce the AI Verification ChecklistApply before any AI-assisted content enters an official system.
6
Expand to additional record typesOnce established for one record type, introduce to other elevated-risk categories.
Implementation note

Start with one record type and one team. Expand from there.
