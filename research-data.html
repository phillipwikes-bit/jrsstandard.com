Research Data Room

A consolidated, de-identified view of everything the JRS platform records: challenge responses, extended reviews, automated engine reviews, poll votes, free-text responses, observation and survey submissions, findings, and the study registry. Each dataset can be exported as CSV or JSON. Names and email addresses are never shown here: contact identifiers stay in private tables. Data is observational and from a validation-phase program; it is not validated and makes no claim of effectiveness. Where participants gave consent, consent flags are included.

▶ Rung 1 · Reproducibility decomposition (latest nightly run)

Reproducibility = how consistently the same record is judged the same way. The nightly probe compares several AI models on each record; the figure is how often they agree, broken out per record below with sample size. When the models are from the same provider this is a strong consistency signal but not independent; when they are from different vendors (a non-Claude provider key is configured) it is independent cross-vendor agreement. Per-condition decomposition is not available because the probe records one overall answer per record, and is not synthesized here.

▶ Reference Panel · Rung 2 accuracy & agreement (computed live) cohort —

For judgment-heavy records there is often no single correct score, only a distribution of defensible expert judgments. The engine (rung 2) is evaluated by whether it behaves like a competent member of that distribution, not by matching one answer key; agreement among the experts is itself a primary metric, reported with its N. Reviewer agreement (reliability, not a rung) measures how often independent reviewers reach the same verdict. The panel is called a benchmark only when expert-to-expert agreement clears the configured threshold, otherwise a reference panel (alignment with informed professional judgment, not correctness against a single truth). Rows marked (demo) are illustrative placeholders, excluded from the figures. Rung 3 (validation: whether a JRS verdict predicts real-world outcomes) is separate and has no data yet.
