Subject: An invitation to co-author a legal article (separate from the review)

Hi Gabriela,

First, thank you for taking on the records review. This note is about something separate, and I want to be clear at the outset: it is not connected to that study or to the records you are working through. It is a distinct piece.

I have nearly finished a legal article, "The Evidentiary Deficit in AI-Assisted Record-Keeping." The draft is attached. It argues that AI-assisted records increasingly read as complete while losing the evidence that would let anyone reconstruct and defend the decision behind them, and it sets out the litigation and regulatory exposure that follows.

The piece is strong on the US side: discovery, the McDonnell Douglas burden-shifting analysis, ESI obligations. What it needs to be complete is a European legal voice, and you are my first choice. Section V opens the European frame, the GDPR accountability principle and the EU AI Act's record-keeping and logging expectations for high-risk uses. I left it deliberately as a skeleton, because the precise contours are yours to draw. That contribution would make you a co-author, and you will see your name already on the attached draft in that spirit.

On placement, I think the natural home is the Oxford Business Law Blog: citable, international, and read by exactly the audience we want. Lexology or JD Supra is a strong second option and reaches the AI-law community you are already recognized in. I am glad to defer to your judgment on venue.

A word on authorship so there is no ambiguity: co-authorship is earned by contribution, so your name goes on when your section does, and none of this is an obligation of the review. If the timing or the fit is not right, no problem at all, and I remain grateful for the review.

Would you be open to it? If so, expand Section V as you see fit, and we will shape the rest together.
