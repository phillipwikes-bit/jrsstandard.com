Hi Maung Myint Swe,

I have long admired your work documenting human rights violations against Rohingya refugee communities, especially your commitment to ethical, survivor-centered documentation and to accountability for displaced populations.

I am facilitating the Justification Review Standard (JRS) Initiative, an independent international effort built on a simple principle: when a consequential decision affects a person's safety, protection, or ability to seek justice, that person deserves to know why it was made, and deserves a fair way to question it. The same principle protects the integrity of the records that document what happened to them.

People deserve decisions they can understand.

People deserve records that can withstand independent review.

People deserve a meaningful opportunity to question decisions that affect their rights, livelihoods, and futures.

That is what JRS is built to protect. As more documentation is drafted with the help of AI, a record can read as complete yet still fail to show, on its own terms, how a conclusion was reached. JRS is a practical way to check whether a record can be independently reconstructed and defended, before it is finalized, so it holds up when accountability depends on it.

I am giving away the JRS Investigator Field Guide to professionals and organizations at no cost, including an international edition suited to human rights and cross-border documentation. There is no sign-up or catch, and you can download it directly here:

https://www.jrsstandard.com/guide

Please feel free to grab it, share it, and pass it to your documentation and research teams.
