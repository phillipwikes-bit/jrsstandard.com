## Post 1

"That's just the way it is."

Every workplace has someone who says it.

A promotion.
A termination.
A hiring decision.
A complaint that quietly disappears.

No explanation. Just... "that's just the way it is."

But what if the person reviewing that decision two years later asks a simple question:

Why?

Can the record answer it? Or does it simply repeat the outcome without showing the reasoning?

That is Decision Reconstruction Risk (DRR): the point where a record can no longer explain how a consequential decision was reached.

AI can draft beautiful documentation. It cannot create reasoning that never existed.

If you have ever investigated a file that "looked complete" but could not answer the obvious questions, you have seen DRR.

When the reasoning disappears, fairness becomes impossible to verify. Good organizations deserve records that can defend good decisions.

## Post 3

"The algorithm is screwing me."

I have heard people blame algorithms. Managers. HR. Recruiters.

Sometimes they are right. Sometimes they are wrong.

But as a former Lead Civil Rights Officer investigating discrimination complaints, I learned something.

The biggest problem usually is not identifying who is responsible.

It is proving what actually happened.

If no one can reconstruct:

- what information existed
- what reasoning was used
- who reviewed it
- why one outcome was chosen over another

then accountability becomes almost impossible.

That is Decision Reconstruction Risk. It affects organizations whether AI was involved or not. AI simply magnifies it.

For investigators, HR professionals, compliance teams, and attorneys, the Investigator Field Guide is free:

## Post 4

"Why does everything I receive from HR sound like a robot wrote it?"

Because sometimes... it did.

That is not automatically the problem.

The real problem is when AI makes every record sound complete while quietly removing the reasoning that makes it defensible.

Years later the file looks polished. But nobody can answer:

Why this employee?
Why this discipline?
Why this promotion?
Why this denial?

The language survived. The reasoning did not.

That is Decision Reconstruction Risk (DRR). The more organizations adopt AI drafting, the more important independent reconstruction becomes.
