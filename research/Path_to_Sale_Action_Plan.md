# JRS / DRR: Path to a Sale (action plan and buyer-conversation timing)

*Private strategy. Sequences the missing gates that move sale probability, and answers the timing question: start buyer conversations now, in two stages, do not wait for publications. Probability figures are reasoned estimates, not measured facts. No long dashes.*

---

## Probability anchors (reasoned estimates, ~12-month sale)

- Today, as-is (no buyer process, pre-revenue, unregistered marks): **~5 to 10%**
- Three levers done (papers, downloads, training), still no buyer process or revenue: **~15 to 25%**
- Three levers + registered marks + real buyer process + one or two paying pilots: **~40 to 55%**

The jump comes from the demand-side gates, not from more artifacts.

---

## Core principle: run everything in parallel

Publications take months. Buyer pipelines take months. Serializing them wastes a quarter. The three levers, the marks, the first paid pilot, and the buyer outreach all run at once. Publications and pilots become momentum you feed into conversations as they land, not a starting gun you wait for.

---

## The buyer-conversation timing rule (the direct answer)

**Do not wait until publications are out. Start now, in two stages.**

**Stage 1 (start immediately): warm, exploratory conversations.**
- Relationship and intel, not a pitch. "I am building X, I would value your perspective on where it fits."
- No data room, no confidential core, NDA before any specifics beyond the public story.
- Purpose: learn what each buyer type would actually pay for, surface interest, and let that intel reprioritize which paper/pilot/evidence matters most. Early interest itself raises your probability and can create competitive tension later.

**Stage 2 (trigger, not a date): formal process.**
- Open it when you have at least: one publication **accepted or in-press** (accepted counts, you do not need it printed), marks **filed**, and one **paying pilot** in hand.
- Then: teaser one-pager, NDA, curated data room (de-identified), targeted outreach to the Stage 1 warm list plus fresh targets.
- Rationale: your first substantive, diligence-grade pitch should withstand scrutiny. Accepted-not-yet-printed is enough proof; waiting for the printed issue is waiting for nothing.

Net: warm conversations begin this month; the formal process begins the moment the first paper is accepted, not when it is published.

## Sequenced plan

### Phase 0 (now, weeks 0 to 4): remove the cheapest gates, warm the pipeline
- File the **JRS and DRR trademarks**. Highest leverage per dollar; anchors the category claim.
- Keep the three levers moving (finish the papers toward submission, drive downloads, grow training completions).
- Stand up a **paying pilot ask**: convert at least one active pilot org to a paid engagement, even a small fee. Willingness to pay is worth more than volume.
- Draft a **private teaser one-pager** (public story only) and a **buyer target list** by archetype (below).
- Begin **Stage 1 warm conversations** with 3 to 5 targets.

### Phase 1 (weeks 4 to 12): convert story into proof
- Land the **first publication acceptance** (FOIL or Business Ethics), and use it as a milestone message to the warm list.
- Close the **first paid pilot**; capture a short reference or outcome.
- Prepare **NDA + data room** (de-identified dataset, reliability results, pilot evidence, IP asset map, transfer-consented list stats). Keep the answer key and scoring out.
- Move warm contacts to substantive talks as the acceptance and pilot land.

### Phase 2 (months 3 to 6): run a real process
- Open the **formal process** with the teaser and targeted outreach.
- Create **competitive tension**: parallel conversations, not one exclusive early suitor.
- Drive toward indications of interest and term sheets.

## Target buyer archetypes

- **GRC / compliance software vendors** (the core commercial fit).
- **E-discovery / legal-tech** (records defensibility, evidence).
- **HR-tech / people-analytics** (adverse-action defensibility).
- **AI-governance platforms** (documentation and accountability layer).
- **Standards bodies / foundations** (mission-aligned home for the community layer; usually lower price, but a credible exit and a category anchor).

---

## Guardrails (these protect the price and the studies)

1. **NDA before specifics.** The public human-rights story is open; the dataset, method, answer key, transfer map, and buyer list are not.
2. **Protect the trade-secret core.** The five-condition scoring and the gold answer key never go into a data room or conveyed code.
3. **Protect the blind.** Nothing shared with a buyer reveals the Arm B method or that reviewers are split into arms.
4. **Do not publish sale intent.** No "for sale" signal on the public site or LinkedIn.
5. **Hold all claims to the completer sample** (8 countries, 5 continents) and the pre-registered reliability numbers.
6. **Do not signal urgency.** Exploratory framing, parallel conversations, no desperation.
