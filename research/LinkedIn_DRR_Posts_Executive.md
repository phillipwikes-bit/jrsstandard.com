## Post 1

Your best manager just resigned.

So did your evidence.

Every decision they made this year is documented. Sort of.

The files say what was decided.

The reasoning lived in their head.

Now a complaint lands on your desk about a termination they approved in March. The file is polished. Professional. AI-drafted.

And it cannot answer one question: why.

That is Decision Reconstruction Risk (DRR): when a record cannot explain, on its own terms, how a consequential decision was reached.

People leave. Records stay.

The only version of a decision your organization gets to keep is the one in the file.
