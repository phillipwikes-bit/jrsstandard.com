## Longer version (original)

Jake,

I read "On Perfection," and I wanted to tell you it stayed with me.

You did the hard thing: you built it from the ground up. Definitions, then axioms, then theorems, rather than starting in the middle the way most writing on this does. The move that landed for me is the one at the center: that perfection and existence exclude each other, and that the first disturbance is not a flaw in a perfect thing but the very thing that makes existence possible. Framing the confusion of the aspirational with the informational as a category mistake is a genuinely clarifying idea, and I appreciated that you closed not on the proof but on what is left once the proof lands: play, boredom, love, song. That is the part most people would have cut, and it is the part that made the whole thing feel true rather than just argued.

I also noticed you wrote the objections section against your own work before anyone else could. That takes a particular kind of honesty, and it is the same instinct that made you a sharp reviewer.

Thank you for sending it my way. It is a real pleasure knowing the kind of mind behind the reviews. I would happily talk it through with you sometime if you ever want a reader to push on it.

Warm regards,
Phillip
