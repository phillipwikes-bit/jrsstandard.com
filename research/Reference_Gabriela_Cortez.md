# Letter of Reference: Gabriela Cortez

*Phillip Wikes · Creator, Justification Review Standard (JRS) · Former Lead Civil Rights Officer, Maryland Commission on Civil Rights · info@jrsstandard.com · jrsstandard.com*

July 17, 2026

**To whom it may concern:**

I am writing to recommend Gabriela Cortez without reservation. I have known and worked with Gabi in two capacities: first as her mentor at the Maryland Commission on Civil Rights, where I trained her in the drafting and review of discrimination complaints, and more recently as an independent reviewer on the international panel assembled to validate the Justification Review Standard.

In her civil rights work, Gabi paired technical skill with a rare sense of accountability. She held that a record must be understandable to the very people it affects, and she practiced that conviction daily: she regularly translated intake records into Spanish so that a language barrier never cost someone clear, honest information about a decision that touched their life. Her documentation was careful, plain, and defensible, the kind of record that holds up when someone later reads it cold. Her professional philosophy, that people and their records alike should "stand on business" and take full responsibility for what they assert, materially shaped my own work.

She is also a genuinely collaborative colleague. She advocated for open communication and for drawing on different cultures and backgrounds, on the sound view that a decision examined by people who see it differently is a decision more likely to survive challenge. That instinct is exactly what a records-review discipline demands.

Most recently, Gabi completed the independent review of all twenty-four records in the JRS international validation study. She worked through the full set with real care and independent judgment, and her contribution stands as part of an international panel of experienced professionals spanning multiple countries, languages, and domains.

I recommend her highly for any role, engagement, or credential that calls for sound judgment, accountable documentation, bilingual client service, and integrity under scrutiny. I am glad to speak further on her behalf at any time.

Sincerely,

Phillip Wikes
Creator, Justification Review Standard (JRS)
