What these are

As AI-assisted drafting enters investigative and administrative documentation, records can read as complete and professional while lacking traceable factual grounding. The basis for a conclusion may live in the author's memory rather than on the page, a distinction that becomes significant when the author is no longer available and the record must be examined on its own.

The Justification Review Standard (JRS) provides a structured way to identify those documentation-traceability gaps before a record is finalized. Each guide walks through the five conditions, the AI-documentation risk patterns, a Record Interrogation Framework, and a one-page reviewer quick reference.

Stated precisely

These guides are supplemental investigative references. They do not constitute legal advice, establish legal sufficiency, or replace applicable investigative procedures. References to specific agencies or statutes describe the contexts in which the method may be applied and do not imply endorsement or affiliation. Applicable jurisdiction, national legislation, and organizational procedures govern.
