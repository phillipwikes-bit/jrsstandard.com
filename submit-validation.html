Validation · Rung 3

Submit a Validated Case

Rung 3 (validation) tests whether a JRS verdict predicts what actually happened to a record when it was challenged. Each case pairs a de-identified record with its documented real-world outcome, so we can compare the JRS read against reality.

Use only public or fully de-identified material. Do not submit any confidential, internal, or non-public record. Good sources: published FOIL appeal determinations, Committee on Open Government opinions, Article 78 and other court rulings, arbitration awards, audit findings, and administrative-hearing decisions where the record was quoted and the outcome is in writing. Remove all names, identifiers, and case numbers that are not part of a public citation.
20 to 8000 characters. This is screened for personal identifiers before it is sent.
A verifiable public reference for the outcome. This is what makes it validation rather than anecdote.
Submitted cases arrive as pending entries for de-identification review before they count toward Rung 3. Validation is domain-specific: a FOIL outcome speaks to records-access decisions, an HR outcome to employment records. Nothing here is published as proof until reviewed.
