Validation · Rung 3

Submit a Validated Case

Rung 3 (validation) tests whether a JRS verdict predicts what actually happened to a record when it was challenged. Each case pairs a de-identified record with its documented real-world outcome, so we can compare the JRS read against reality.

How many to aim for: a first set of 5 cases, then 20 to 30 in total, entered in small batches over time. There is no need to do them all at once, and your work saves as you go.

Quick eligibility check · read before you start

A case belongs in this study only if you can answer yes to all three questions. If any answer is no, do not enter it.

1
Is it public? A published decision, opinion, ruling, or award that anyone could look up. Never an internal company file, even with the names removed.
2
Does it show the record? The decision quotes the actual record, or the part that mattered: a termination, a write-up, an investigation note, a determination.
3
Is the outcome written down? The decision states what happened when the record was challenged: upheld, overturned, failed an audit, and so on.

When in doubt, leave it out. Your own workplace records do not qualify, no matter how they are edited.

Good public sources. Published FOIL appeal determinations, Committee on Open Government opinions, Article 78 and other court rulings, labor arbitration awards, unemployment appeal decisions, EEOC determinations, audit findings, and administrative-hearing decisions where the record was quoted and the outcome is in writing. Remove all names, identifiers, and case numbers that are not part of a public citation.
20 to 8000 characters. This is screened for personal identifiers before it is sent.
A verifiable public reference for the outcome. This is what makes it validation rather than anecdote.
Submitted cases arrive as pending entries for de-identification review before they count toward Rung 3. Validation is domain-specific: a FOIL outcome speaks to records-access decisions, an HR outcome to employment records. Nothing here is published as proof until reviewed.
