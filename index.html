Justification Review Standard (JRS™)

A rigorous, repeatable standard for evaluating identifiable support for conclusions, claims, and decisions in organizational documentation.

The JRS Process

Step 1: Identify the core claim or conclusion.

Step 2: Locate all referenced or implied evidence within the document.

Step 3: Assess traceability, specificity, and sufficiency of support.

Step 4: Assign a Justification Level (JL-0 through JL-4).

Evaluation Criteria

  • Traceability — Can the claim be directly linked to specific evidence?
  • Specificity — Is the supporting data concrete and unambiguous?
  • Sufficiency — Is the evidence adequate for the strength of the claim?
  • Independence — Is circular reasoning or self-reference avoided?
  • Verifiability — Could an independent reviewer validate the support?

Justification Levels (JL)

JL-4 — Fully supported with primary, verifiable evidence

JL-3 — Strong indirect or multiple corroborating references

JL-2 — Plausible support with some gaps

JL-1 — Weak or anecdotal support

JL-0 — Unsupported assertion / opinion presented as fact

Examples

Example 1 — JL-4 (Strong)

Claim: “Q3 revenue grew 18% year-over-year.”
Support: Appendix A, page 7 contains audited financial statements from Deloitte showing exact figure and reconciliation.

→ Justification Level: JL-4
Example 2 — JL-1 (Weak)

Claim: “Our customers are highly satisfied with the new interface.”
Support: “Feedback has been positive.”

→ Justification Level: JL-1
Example 3 — JL-0 (Unsupported)

Claim: “This initiative will deliver 10x ROI within 12 months.”
Support: None provided.

→ Justification Level: JL-0
