Justification Review Standard

A pre-save documentation control for AI-generated and human-authored records.
Standard ID
JRS-STD-001
Version
1.0
Effective Date
June 2026
Review Cycle
Annual
Status
Issued
Scope

This standard applies to documentation generated by AI systems and human operators in employment, housing, healthcare, public accommodations, and compliance settings.

It is executed before any record is finalized, stored, or relied upon as the basis for a decision affecting an individual's rights or interests.

Context

AI-assisted documentation introduces evidentiary risk.

Records get saved. Later, they have to be defended in audits, in litigation, and in regulatory review.

A justification that cannot stand on its own creates risk the moment it enters the record.
Standard Requirement

Before any AI-generated or human-authored justification is finalized, it must meet three conditions:

1.  The justification stands on its own, without relying on the model, the prompt, or the operator.

2.  It is grounded in something observable, such as a fact, an artifact, or a verifiable source.

3.  It is clearly explainable, so a reviewer outside the original context can understand it.

If any condition fails, do not finalize.

Verification Check

Does it stand on its own?

What is it based on?

Would someone else understand it without context?
