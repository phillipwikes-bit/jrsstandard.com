Justification Review Standard Documentation Review Process Overview The Justification Review Standard establishes a review process for evaluating whether organizational documentation contains an identifiable basis for conclusions and documented decisions. This standard applies to organizational documentation used in employment, administrative, or compliance review processes. The review process is designed to support organizational review practices while acknowledging operational constraints. Definition Justification Review Standard: A documentation review process that evaluates whether organizational records contain observable information supporting conclusions and decisions. The process evaluates whether records are independently understandable and whether the basis for conclusions can be identified during later review. Review Conditions Observable Information Documentation must contain specific, identifiable information that supports the decision. Observable information may include documented observations, interactions, or structured feedback where appropriately identified. Independently Understandable Records A reviewer should be able to understand the basis for conclusions without requiring additional context or supplementary explanation. Identifiable Basis for Conclusions The foundation for conclusions or decisions must be traceable within the record itself. Conclusions should not depend on unstated assumptions or undocumented context. Operational Constraints Application may involve incomplete records, conflicting accounts, operational time pressures, or qualitative information requiring reviewer judgment. The process acknowledges these constraints without eliminating the requirement for identifiable support. Review Limitations The review process is not designed to enable procedural compliance without substantive support. Specifically, the process does not: • Sanitize poor documentation through procedural compliance • Enable box-checking without meaningful review • Substitute procedural compliance for substantive support • Create the appearance of rigor where evidence is absent Instead, the process identifies where documentation lacks identifiable basis for conclusions and distinguishes between wording quality and evidentiary support. Review Procedure The review process applies the following procedure: 01 Identify the Conclusion or Decision Determine what specific conclusion or decision the documentation supports. 02 Locate Observable Support Identify specific, observable information within the record that supports the conclusion. 03 Evaluate Independent Understandability Assess whether a reviewer unfamiliar with the situation could understand the basis for the conclusion using only the record itself. 04 Assess Completeness Determine whether the record contains sufficient information for later review. 05 Document Limitations Acknowledge any incomplete information, conflicting accounts, or qualitative judgments that may affect the record's clarity. Application The review process applies to organizational documentation across multiple contexts: • Performance Documentation: Employee evaluations, feedback, and performance-related decisions. • Hiring Documentation: Recruitment records, candidate evaluation, and hiring decisions. • Termination Documentation: Records supporting employment termination, including disciplinary action or termination documentation. • Administrative Records: Any organizational documentation used in employment, administrative, or compliance review processes. Secondary review may involve HR, compliance, legal, or supervisory personnel depending on documentation type and organizational process. AI-Assisted Documentation The review process applies to AI-assisted documentation before integration into organizational records. Review personnel should: • Verify factual accuracy and identifiable support in AI-generated text • Ensure AI-drafted language reflects documented events and interactions • Confirm AI-assisted text does not overstate certainty or evidentiary support Materials The standard and reference materials are intended for organizational and educational review use.
