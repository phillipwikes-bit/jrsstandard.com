Justification Review Standard (JRS™)

Documentation Review Process
Version
1.0
Effective
May 2026
Overview

The Justification Review Standard establishes a review process for evaluating whether organizational documentation contains identifiable support for conclusions or decisions.

This standard applies to organizational documentation used in employment, administrative, or compliance review processes. The review process is designed to support organizational review practices while acknowledging operational constraints.

Definition

Justification Review Standard (JRS™): A documentation review process used to evaluate whether organizational records are independently understandable and contain an identifiable basis for conclusions during later review.

Review Conditions

Before a record is finalized, documentation should satisfy the following conditions:

01
Independently Understandable Records
A reviewer should be able to identify the basis for conclusions without requiring additional context or supplementary explanation.
02
Observable Information
Documentation must contain specific, identifiable information supporting conclusions or decisions. Observable information may include documented observations, interactions, structured feedback, or other referenced supporting documentation.
03
Identifiable Basis for Conclusions
The foundation for conclusions or decisions must be traceable within the record itself. Conclusions should not depend on unstated assumptions or undocumented context.
Context

Records may later be reviewed during audits, investigations, escalation processes, disputes, or administrative review.

Documentation that does not identify the basis for conclusions may be interpreted inconsistently during later review.
Review Procedure
Identify the Conclusion or Decision
Locate Observable Support
Evaluate Independent Understandability
Assess Identifiable Support
Document Limitations
Application

This standard applies to administrative documentation, including but not limited to:

Performance Reviews
Disciplinary Documentation
Hiring & Rejection Documentation
Termination Documentation
Performance Improvement Plans
Accommodation Documentation

These examples are illustrative and not exhaustive.

Operational Constraints

Application may involve incomplete records, conflicting accounts, operational time pressures, or qualitative information requiring reviewer judgment.

Observable information may include documented interactions, structured feedback, qualitative observations, or referenced supporting documentation where appropriately identified.

Application depth may vary based on workflow, record type, and operational constraints.

Review Limitations

The review process is not intended to substitute procedural formality for identifiable evidentiary support. It does not:

Sanitize poor documentation through procedural compliance
Enable box-checking without meaningful review
Create the appearance of rigor where evidence is absent

Instead, the process identifies where documentation lacks an identifiable basis for conclusions and distinguishes between wording quality and evidentiary support.

AI-Assisted Documentation

When AI tools are used to draft or refine documentation, the underlying observable information must still satisfy the review conditions. AI-generated language should not be treated as independent evidentiary support absent identifiable underlying information. Review responsibility remains with organizational personnel regardless of drafting method.

Governance Compatibility

JRS™ is intended to operate within existing organizational documentation, compliance, investigation, legal review, and AI governance environments.
