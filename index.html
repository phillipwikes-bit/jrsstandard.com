JRS™ Documentation Review Process

A documentation review process for employment documentation and organizational review environments.
Version
1.0
Effective
May 2026
Purpose

This documentation review process is intended for use before employment documentation is finalized within organizational systems.

The review process is intended to reduce ambiguity in written records before finalization by reinforcing identifiable support for conclusions, independently understandable documentation, and reviewable reasoning.

Review Conditions

Before a record is finalized, the documentation review process applies three review conditions.

01
Independently understandable
The record can be understood without reliance on the original drafter, prompt history, or surrounding context.
02
Identifiable basis
Conclusions are tied to identifiable information, documentation, events, or observable support.
03
Reviewable reasoning
A reviewer outside the original context can identify the basis for conclusions during later review.
Context

Records may later be reviewed during audits, investigations, escalation processes, disputes, or administrative review.

Documentation that does not identify the basis for conclusions may be interpreted inconsistently during later review.
Application
Performance Reviews
Disciplinary Documentation
Hiring & Rejection Documentation
Termination Documentation
Performance Improvement Plans
Accommodation Documentation
Implementation Constraints

Application may involve incomplete records, conflicting accounts, operational time pressures, or qualitative information requiring reviewer judgment.

Observable information may include documented interactions, structured feedback, qualitative observations, or referenced supporting documentation where appropriately identified.

Application depth may vary based on workflow, record type, and operational constraints.

Governance Compatibility

The documentation review process is intended to operate within existing organizational documentation, compliance, investigation, legal review, and AI governance environments.

Limitations of Review Process
Does not determine employment outcomes
Does not replace legal review
Does not eliminate subjective judgment
Does not establish legal sufficiency
Does not replace organizational policy obligations
